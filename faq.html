What is my take home pay and how is it different from salary?

Your take home pay is your salary after tax and national insurance contributions (in the UK). In other words it is the sum that is paid to you in your payslip. You should always budget based on your take home pay and not on your job's advertised salary.

What is budgeting and how can I learn more?

A budget is your plan for how you are going to allocate your money to different areas of your life, whether that is paying for expenses, paying off debt, investing or saving. See Martin Lewis's helpful article for more details.
